Commodore 64

August 1982

Developed by Commodore International, this 8-bit home computer brought an entirely new dimension to the world of personal computing with its advanced sound and graphic capabilities. It went on to become one of the best-selling single computer models of all time.

The year 1982 saw the birth of this iconic machine which set a record for selling an unprecedented 17 million units. The Commodore 64, with its charming brown casing, distinctive keyboard, and its in-built RF modulator, was an introduction for many to the realm of home computing.

Reviving the charm of the early computing era, this well-preserved Commodore 64 invites you to delve into over 10,000 games and software titles, unrivaled in the 8-bit era. It brought not only entertainment into homes but served as a starting point for a generation of programmers.

Imagine the thrill of maneuvering pixels across the screen in the classic game of Space Invaders, the rhythmic clacking of keys as you tap out lines of BASIC code, the satisfaction of seeing your first program spring to life - this is what the Commodore 64 offers.

Welcome back the golden era of home computing with your Commodore 64.
